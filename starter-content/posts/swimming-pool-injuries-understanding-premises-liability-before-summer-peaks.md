# How to Write a Homepage That Converts Visitors Into Customers

Most business websites have the same problem: they talk about themselves instead of talking to their customers. Here is how to fix that.

## Start With the Customer Problem

Your headline should answer one question: what problem do you solve? Not who you are, not how long you have been in business — the problem you solve and who you solve it for.

## Lead With a Clear Value Proposition

Within 5 seconds of landing on your page, a visitor should know what you do, who it is for, and why they should choose you. Use plain language. Avoid jargon.

## Use Social Proof Early

Testimonials, client logos, review counts, and case study results build trust fast. Place social proof above the fold wherever possible.

## One Clear Call to Action

Every homepage should have one primary call to action. Not three, not five — one. Make it obvious, make it specific, and make it easy to find.

## Make It Fast

Page speed directly impacts conversion rate and SEO rankings. Compress images, minimize JavaScript, and use a performant hosting setup.
